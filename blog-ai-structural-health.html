Across Africa, infrastructure is expanding rapidly ‚Äî from roads and bridges to high-rise buildings. However, maintaining structural safety in these assets remains a critical challenge. Artificial Intelligence (AI) offers a powerful solution: continuous, data-driven monitoring that detects risks before they become disasters.

What Is Structural Health Monitoring (SHM)?

SHM is the use of sensors, data, and algorithms to assess the condition of structures in real-time. Traditionally, engineers relied on periodic manual inspections, but AI now enables automated analysis of vibration, strain, and temperature data from sensors embedded in bridges or buildings.

How AI Improves Monitoring

  • Predictive Maintenance: Machine learning models forecast potential damage or material fatigue.
  • Anomaly Detection: AI detects structural changes invisible to human inspectors.
  • Automated Alerts: Systems can send early warnings via mobile dashboards.
  • Cost Reduction: Less manual inspection means lower maintenance costs.

Applications in Rwanda and East Africa
