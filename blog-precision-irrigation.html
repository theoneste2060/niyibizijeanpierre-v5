Precision Irrigation: Using AI and IoT to Save Water in African Farms

Water is one of the most valuable yet limited resources for African agriculture. As climate change increases droughts and irregular rainfall, farmers need new ways to manage irrigation efficiently. Precision irrigation, powered by Artificial Intelligence (AI) and the Internet of Things (IoT), offers a sustainable solution.

How Precision Irrigation Works

Precision irrigation systems use sensors, satellite data, and machine learning to determine when and how much to water crops. Soil moisture sensors, temperature probes, and weather stations send data to an AI platform that calculates the exact irrigation schedule for each field.

  • Soil Sensors: Measure real-time moisture levels.
  • Weather Data: Predicts rainfall and evaporation rates.
  • AI Analysis: Determines optimal water quantity and timing.
  • Automated Valves: Deliver water only when needed.
